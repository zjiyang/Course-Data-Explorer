# Team Agreement

## Communication

**How will you communicate?**  
- Primary: Texting and Audio Call (WeChat)  
- Secondary: If something is too complex to resolve by messaging/call, we will discuss it in the next scheduled meeting or arrange an ad-hoc meeting (remote or in-person) as needed.

**How quickly will you respond to messages from your partner?**  
- We aim to respond **within 6 hours on the same day**.  
- If someone is in class / working, they should send a short “received, will reply by ___” message.

**What types of updates require immediate communication vs. asynchronous communication?**  
Immediate communication (as soon as possible):
- Risk of missing a deadline (cannot finish a task on time)
- Blockers (cannot proceed due to missing info/access/bug)
- Major changes to scope (feature removal/addition that affects other work)
- Merge conflicts or issues that break the build/tests
- A PR that blocks the teammate’s progress and needs prompt review/decision

Asynchronous communication (can wait until next meeting / within 6 hours):
- Routine progress updates
- Small refactors, minor UI tweaks, formatting changes
- Questions that do not block other tasks immediately

## Meetings

**Will you meet in person or remotely?**  
- Default: **Remote** (WeChat call).  
- In-person is optional if both agree and it helps productivity.

**How often will you meet each week (outside of the weekly TA meeting)?**  
- **3 times per week**

**How long will your meetings be?**  
- Usually **30 minutes**, adjustable based on workload.

Additional clarifiers:
- **How will you schedule meetings?**  
  - We schedule meetings right after each new assignment/project milestone is released, and confirm times via texting.
  - Preferred times are flexible; we will prioritize weekday evenings when possible.

- **What happens if someone needs to miss or reschedule?**  
  - Notify the other teammate ASAP.  
  - We will reschedule to a new time **within 24 hours**, based on earliest common availability.

## Contribution and Workload Expectations

**What grade are you aiming for on the project?**  
- Our goal is **A-range target (90%+)**.

**What happens if teammates want different target grades?**  
- We will discuss trade-offs early (scope vs. polish vs. time).  
- If one teammate targets a higher grade, we will align on a “must-have vs. nice-to-have” plan so expectations are clear.

**Is it acceptable for one team member to do more work to achieve a higher grade?**  
- Yes. If a teammate wants a higher grade and is willing to contribute more, that is acceptable, as long as responsibilities are transparent and agreed in advance.

**What factors might affect your ability to work on the project?**  
- Other course workload, exams, and personal obligations.

**Which weeks will you have reduced capacity?**  
- Midterm-heavy weeks (we will identify them in advance based on each course schedule).

## Implementation and Workflow

**Where will you record task responsibilities?**  
- GitHub Issues + a GitHub Project board (To Do / In Progress / Review / Done)

**How will you communicate deadlines for your tasks?**  
- Each Issue includes:
  - Owner
  - Due date (target)
  - Definition of Done (DoD)
- We also confirm deadlines verbally during meetings and summarize in a short text message after each meeting.

**What happens if you cannot meet a deadline you set?**  
- Notify the teammate as soon as risk is identified (do not wait until the deadline).  
- If we expect to miss a due date by **more than 24 hours**, we must notify immediately and re-plan.  
- Propose one of:
  - a new realistic deadline
  - a smaller-scope version (MVP first)
  - requesting help / handing off part of the task

**How will you show your partner that your work is complete?**  
- Open a Pull Request (PR) with:
  - clear description of changes
  - screenshots / demo notes if UI-related
  - how to test (steps/commands)
  - linked Issue(s)

**Do you want to enable linting? If so, will you disable any default rules?**  
- Yes. We will enable **ESLint + Prettier** for our TypeScript codebase.  
- We prefer to keep default rules; if a rule slows development or causes excessive noise, we will discuss and adjust together.

**How often will you push changes to the repository?**  
- Push at least **after each work session** (or daily).  
- **No direct pushes to `main`**; we will use branches + PRs and keep `main` stable.

**How long should code reviews take?**  
- Review PRs within **48 hours**, ideally within **24 hours** when deadlines are near or when the PR blocks other work.

**How large should pull requests be?**  
- Prefer small PRs: **one feature / one bugfix**, and ideally not extremely large.  
- We prefer PRs under ~**500 lines of change** when possible.  
- If a PR is large due to necessity, include extra explanation and testing notes.
